## A lot of tools, none fits me
When I started storytelling, more than 15 years ago now that I actually think about it, I used pen & paper. It held everything — notes, plots, story arcs, NPCs, maps, all of it. Great, until you wanted to find something quickly, or, like me, couldn't read your own handwriting a week later.

So I tried more things than I'd like to admit. OneNote (ewww, Microslop). Wiki. WorldAnvil. Google Drive docs. Goodnotes. All of them, same story: not available everywhere, not editable on every device, no way to keep track of what lives where, no linking between important parts, no working calendar, no name generator, and still, somehow, a sticky note or magnetic board hanging around for initiative tracking. Add a ton of paper hauled on my back, and you get the idea.

Eventually I found the "professional" tools built for exactly this. Problem is, they all commit hard to one system, and I don't play one system.

Most of my table time goes to nWoD — Vampire the Requiem, Mage the Awakening, Hunter the Vigil — but I'll happily jump into FATE, City of Mist, Shadowrun, Hellboy, GURPS, Dračí Doupě 2… you name it. And those systems don't just look different, they need completely different tracking, completely different notes.

So this year, I surrendered. Started building my own thing. Still early days — everything about it can and will change — but there's a PoC, and it's on GitHub if you want to poke around.

Here's where I want it to end up:

Multi-campaign ready
DB powered (the big to-do)
Editable on the go, in Markdown
Accessible from anywhere, behind a login
Interactive pieces: a calendar (real-world or fully custom, your call), progress/doom counters, notes shareable with players or kept DM-only, an initiative tracker, a name generator (ideally with a local LLM behind it, plus a sample sheet for offline use)
A player character section, because nobody remembers a PC's backstory past session one
Catppuccin themed, obviously

Summer's busy, but I'll keep you posted.
