## When community becomes a business model

Blood on the Clocktower is a game you play with strangers and with friends, often at the same table, on schedules that never quite line up. That's the actual hard part of running these games regularly — not the mechanics, just getting enough people in the same place at the same time. And more often than not, the strangers turn into friends somewhere around the third or fourth session — which is probably the real reason people keep showing up.

Which is also, I think, why the community around it feels a lot like the open-source world — GitHub, Linux, Creative Commons licenses — people building and sharing because they actually care, not because there's a business plan behind it. Shoutout to Zdunda and his [Czech BOTC translation tool](https://botc.zdenekzert.net/)!

That said, me and a few others from the community aren't really thrilled with the direction a Czech project Bluffish seems to be heading — organizing turning into a paid thing, with subscriptions apparently next. Not outrage, just: not a fan of where that road goes.

So, free planner it is. Mine, this time.

The idea: something you can open from your phone or your PC without thinking twice, that actually tracks who's coming to an event instead of a group chat message nobody replies to, or facebook event that becomes invisible at some point, puts the venue on a map instead of a paragraph of directions, and lets people argue about which script to play the way they would in a Facebook event thread — except it's actually built for that, not repurposed for it.

What it needs to do:

* Work equally well on phone and PC
* Accounts, so people show up as themselves and not "guest_47"
* RSVP tracking — know how many people are actually coming, not guessing
* Map integration for the venue
* A script-discussion thread per event, before anyone shows up
* Weather forecast built in, because half these events are outdoors and nobody checks until it's too late
* A link straight to ClockTracker — whoever built that deserves the traffic, not a reason to reinvent it badly

And no, for once — not Catppuccin. Purple and parchment, closer to what Pandemonium actually put out for the game itself. Some things deserve their own theme.

Free, or close to it. More once there's something to actually show.
