Every designer has a graveyard of abandoned portfolios. Mine has four headstones now, and this one's the first I actually like enough to keep.

The first version was an HTML page pretending to be a website. The second was too minimal — plus I didn't really have anything to show or discuss, or I did, but didn't really want to. The third ended up more like a design/psychological study of how deep the rabbit hole leads if you decide to make a command-line-only navigation and interaction webpage.

What's different this time

I stopped trying to make it look like everyone else's portfolio. This one runs on ```Catppuccin``` — Mocha by default, with a Latte toggle for anyone who prefers their websites like their coffee: with milk. I don't.

It's also finally honest about what I actually make: half of it is code, a good chunk is design proposals nobody asked for, there's some art, 3D prints (hidden through the blog section), a couple of terrible-on-purpose game-wannabes because I did some courses without ever having my own idea for a game, and — eventually — music and poetry, once I get cocky enough to read my own notes scattered everywhere over the years.

I have a homelab now, that's what's keeping this up, and I want a place to put my apps as well.

Also, I want to motivate my dearest Mája — wife-to-be — to make one for herself, and put a locally running LLM to good use while she's at it.

Anyway. If you're reading this, the site shipped. That's something I can be proud of — maybe you'll find something interesting here.
